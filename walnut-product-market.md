<p><p>The walnut product market can be categorized into four main types based on species: Trachycaryon, Cardiocayon, Rhysocaryon, and Juglans. Trachycaryon encompasses varieties known for their robust kernels, ideal for snacking and culinary uses. Cardiocayon features walnuts prized for their flavor and oil content. Rhysocaryon includes those with unique texture and taste profiles, appealing to gourmet markets. Juglans, the most common, includes English and Black walnuts, widely consumed and used in baking, cooking, and oil production globally.</p></p>
