<p><p>The industrial gas sensors market comprises several key types, each serving distinct functions. Electrochemical sensors detect gases through chemical reactions, providing sensitive measurements for toxic and combustible gases. Semiconductor sensors utilize changes in electrical resistance to identify gas presence, often used for quick detection. Infrared sensors measure gas concentrations by analyzing absorbed infrared light, effective for organic compounds. Photoionization detectors (PIDs) ionize gas molecules using UV light, enabling quick and accurate detection of volatile organic compounds and hazardous gases.</p></p>
